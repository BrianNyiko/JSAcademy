Our Story: From a Grade 11 Breakthrough to a Maths Revolution

Back in Grade 11, John Squad, a passionate student at Gasekhukhune Refilwe Secondary School, faced the same struggle many South African learners know too well: trigonometry. Determined to crack its code, John didn’t just study—he innovated. By simplifying complex concepts into an intuitive system, he created the JS Method (J for John, S for Squad), a game-changer that made trigonometry feel like a breeze.

What started as a personal breakthrough became a lifeline for his peers. John’s method helped classmates excel, and word spread fast. By 2018, he began tutoring formally, sharing the JS Method with students at Gasekhukhune Refilwe Secondary. Since then, John and JSAcademy have reached 97 schools across Limpopo, transforming how thousands of Grade 8-12 students tackle Mathematics.

The JS Method: Making Trigonometry Simple

Trigonometry doesn’t have to be a nightmare. The JS Method, crafted by John Squad, breaks down sine, cosine, and tangent into a straightforward, memorable system that clicks for students. Developed when John was just 16, this method has proven its worth, helping learners not just pass but dominate their Maths exams.

Unlike traditional approaches that drown you in formulas, the JS Method uses intuitive patterns and practical shortcuts, making trigonometry accessible even for those who’ve struggled before. It’s not just a technique—it’s a mindset that builds confidence and clarity.
